  \begin{block}{Introduction}

      \begin{itemize}
      \item Birds are a vital part of the agricultural and horticultural ecosystem since they help in pest control, pollination, and seed dispersal. 
      \item However, they can also inflict damage to the crops by feeding on the grains, fruits, and seeds reducing the overall production.
      \item To solve the bird-control problem, farmers must be able to identify and differentiate between the destructive and necessary species of birds.
      \item Varying weather conditions, bio-acoustic noise, and different sounds made by the same bird species such as calling, singing, whistle make it challenging to classify them.
    \end{itemize} 

    \begin{figure}
        \centering
        \includegraphics{logos/birds.jpg}
        \caption{House sparrow and House finch from a distance look alike visually}
    \end{figure}

  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Project Goal}

    To create a set of field deployable devices to detect destructive crop raiders and classify them by their species in a reliable and cost-effective manner

    \end{block}

    \begin{block}{Requirements}
    
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Classify at least two distinct species
      \item Maximum price per unit inclusive of all costs should be less than \$50
      \item Operates for at least 3 days without a recharge
      \item Classifier should have an accuracy of at least 70\% on available test data (xeno-canto)
    \end{itemize}

  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Design Choice: Audio Based Approach}

    Audio was chosen as the method of detection over a visual approach or an audio-visual approach since:

    \begin{itemize}
      \item Lower cost and higher accessibility of high-quality microphones
      \item Lower power usage due to less data bandwidth of audio data
      \item Omni-directional and functional in varied environment conditions
    \end{itemize}

  \end{block}

\end{column}
